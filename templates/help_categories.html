Using Categories in Kryptopedia

Understanding Categories

What are Categories?

Categories in Kryptopedia help organize articles into related groups, making it easier for readers to find information on specific topics. They function like tags or labels, but with a hierarchical structure that creates a taxonomy of knowledge.

A well-categorized article helps readers:

  • Discover related content
  • Understand the context of the information
  • Navigate through similar topics

Category Structure

Kryptopedia uses a flat category structure, where each category stands on its own. Categories appear at the bottom of articles and link to category pages that list all articles in that category.

Categories on an article about Bitcoin might include:
  • Cryptocurrency
  • Blockchain Technology
  • Digital Assets

Adding Categories to Articles

How to Add Categories

You can add categories to an article during creation or editing:

  1. In the article editor, locate the "Categories" field
  2. Enter category names, separated by commas
  3. Categories will be automatically linked when the article is saved
When editing an article about machine learning algorithms, you might add these categories:

Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Algorithms
Tip: Use existing categories when possible to maintain consistency. You can browse existing categories by visiting the Categories page.

Category Naming Conventions

Follow these guidelines when adding categories:

  • Use title case for category names (e.g., "Machine Learning" instead of "machine learning")
  • Be specific but not overly narrow
  • Avoid duplicate or redundant categories
  • Use plural forms for most categories (e.g., "Cryptocurrencies" instead of "Cryptocurrency")
Note: Very specific categories with only one article are generally not useful. Instead, use broader categories that can include multiple related articles.

Category Best Practices

How Many Categories to Use

Most articles should have between 2-5 categories. Too few categories may make an article difficult to find, while too many can dilute its categorization and make navigation less effective.

Good example: An article on "Ethereum Smart Contracts" might use categories: Ethereum, Smart Contracts, Blockchain Applications, Decentralized Finance.

Poor example: The same article with too many categories: Ethereum, Smart Contracts, Blockchain, Cryptocurrency, Programming, Solidity, Web3, DApps, NFTs, Tokens, Digital Assets, Blockchain Applications, DeFi, Finance, Technology.

Choosing the Right Categories

When selecting categories for an article, consider:

  • Relevance: The category should be directly related to the article's main topic
  • Specificity: Include both general and specific categories when appropriate
  • Consistency: Use the same categories for similar articles

For example, an article about a specific programming language like Python should include:

  • The general category "Programming Languages"
  • More specific categories like "Scripting Languages" or "Object-Oriented Languages" if relevant

Advanced Category Usage

Creating New Categories

New categories are created automatically when you add them to an article. Before creating a new category, consider:

  • Is there already a similar category that could be used instead?
  • Will this category be useful for multiple articles?
  • Is the category name clear and follows the naming conventions?
Note: If you're an editor or administrator, you can also manage categories directly through the category administration interface.

Category Maintenance

Editors and administrators regularly review and maintain the category system to ensure it remains useful and organized. This includes:

  • Merging similar or duplicate categories
  • Renaming categories for clarity or consistency
  • Removing rarely used or overly specific categories

If you notice issues with the category system, please contact an administrator with your suggestions.
