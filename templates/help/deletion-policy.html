Deletion Process

1

Consider Alternatives First

Can the article be improved, edited, or merged instead of deleted? Try to fix minor issues before requesting deletion.

2

Review Deletion Criteria

Ensure the article meets specific deletion criteria. Personal disagreement is not sufficient grounds for deletion.

3

Create Deletion Request

Use the deletion request template below. Provide specific criteria, evidence, and consider alternatives.

4

Community Discussion

The community discusses the request, votes, and provides input based on the deletion criteria.

5

Resolution

The article author or an administrator resolves the discussion based on community input and deletion criteria.

Deletion Request Template

Use this template when requesting article deletion to ensure all necessary information is provided:

**Article:** [Article Title and Link] **Deletion Criterion:** [Specific reason from criteria above] **Severity Level:** [Level 1-4 based on guidelines above] **Evidence:** [Specific examples or policy violations] **Alternatives Considered:** [What else could be done instead?] **Additional Notes:** [Any other relevant information]
Tips for Good Deletion Requests:
  • Be specific about which criterion applies
  • Provide concrete examples of problems
  • Consider whether improvement is possible
  • Focus on content, not the contributor
  • Cite specific policies when applicable

Examples

Good Deletion Request Example

Article: "Voiced upper-pharyngeal affricate"
Deletion Criterion: Copyright infringement (Policy Violation)
Severity Level: Level 4 (Critical Issues)
Evidence: This whole article matches another website online being: encyclopedia.com/page/Voiced_upper-pharyngeal_affricate
Alternatives Considered: It could perhaps be rewritten in our own words, or we could give credit.
Additional Notes: The rest of the author's articles are copyright infringment to the same website.

Poor Deletion Request Example

Article: "Climate Change Effects on Agriculture"
Deletion Criterion: I disagree with the conclusions
Evidence: The article presents views I don't agree with
Why This Is Poor: Personal disagreement is not a valid deletion criterion. Controversial topics can remain if they're encyclopedic and well-sourced.

Appeals Process

If Your Article Was Deleted

  1. Review the deletion reason - Understand exactly why it was removed
  2. Address the issues - Fix the problems that led to deletion
  3. Request undeletion - Provide evidence that issues are resolved
  4. Community review - Allow the community to assess improvements

If You Disagree with a Deletion Decision

  1. Review the deletion discussion - Understand the reasoning
  2. Present new evidence - Provide information not previously considered
  3. Request community review - Ask for a new discussion
  4. Appeal to administrators - If you believe the process wasn't followed

Community Guidelines

When participating in deletion discussions:

  • Focus on the content, not the contributor
  • Cite specific policies and provide evidence
  • Consider alternatives to deletion when possible
  • Be respectful and constructive in discussions
  • Base decisions on criteria, not personal preferences
