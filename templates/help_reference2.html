Adding References to Kryptopedia Articles

Introduction to References

References and citations are crucial for establishing the credibility and reliability of articles in Kryptopedia. They provide readers with sources to verify information and explore topics in greater depth.

This guide explains how to properly add and format references in your articles.

Why references matter: In a collaborative knowledge base, proper citation is essential for maintaining accuracy and allowing readers to verify claims. References help build trust with readers and enhance the quality of the wiki as a whole.

Basic Reference Structure

Inline Citations

Inline citations are used to indicate which specific statements are supported by which sources. Here's how to add them:

This is a statement that needs a citation.<sup><a href="#ref1" id="cite1">[1]</a></sup> Another claim requiring verification.<sup><a href="#ref2" id="cite2">[2]</a></sup>
This is a statement that needs a citation.[1] Another claim requiring verification.[2]

The href="#ref1" points to the full reference in the references section, while the id="cite1" allows the reader to jump back to the citation from the references section.

References Section

At the end of your article, include a "References" section that lists all the sources cited in the article:

<h2>References</h2> <ol class="references"> <li id="ref1">Author, A. (2021). Title of the article. <em>Journal Name</em>, 10(2), 30-45. <a href="#cite1">↩</a></li> <li id="ref2">Smith, J. (2022). <em>Book Title</em>. Publisher. <a href="#cite2">↩</a></li> </ol>

References

  1. Author, A. (2021). Title of the article. Journal Name, 10(2), 30-45. ↩
  2. Smith, J. (2022). Book Title. Publisher. ↩

The id="ref1" allows the reader to jump to the full reference from the citation, while the <a href="#cite1">↩</a> lets them jump back to where the citation appears in the text.

Complete Example

Here's a complete example showing how inline citations connect to the references section:

Blockchain technology has transformed the financial industry[1]. Research shows that decentralized systems can reduce transaction costs by up to 40%[2].

References

  1. Nakamoto, S. (2008). Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System. https://bitcoin.org/bitcoin.pdf
  2. Johnson, E. et al. (2021). Cost Efficiency in Decentralized Financial Systems. Journal of Blockchain Economics, 3(2), 112-128.

Citation Formats

Academic Sources

For academic sources like journal articles, books, and conference papers, use a standard citation format like APA or MLA. Here are examples in APA format:

  • Journal Article:
    Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. Title of Journal, volume(issue), page range. DOI or URL
  • Book:
    Author, A. A. (Year). Title of book. Publisher.
  • Book Chapter:
    Author, A. A. (Year). Title of chapter. In E. Editor (Ed.), Title of book (pp. page range). Publisher.
  • Conference Paper:
    Author, A. A. (Year, Month). Title of paper. Paper presented at Conference Name, Location.

Web Sources

For online articles, websites, and other web sources:

  • Web Article:
    Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. Website Name. URL
  • Blog Post:
    Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of post [Blog post]. Blog Name. URL
  • Website:
    Organization Name. (Year). Page title. Website Name. URL
Important: Always include the date you accessed web sources if they might change over time: "Retrieved Month Day, Year, from URL"

Specific to Cryptocurrency and Blockchain

Some sources that are common in the cryptocurrency and blockchain space:

  • Whitepapers:
    Author or Project Name. (Year). Title of whitepaper. URL
  • GitHub Repositories:
    Developer/Organization. (Year). Repository name [Source code]. GitHub. URL
  • Technical Documentation:
    Project Name. (Year). Title of documentation. URL

Best Practices

When to Cite

Always include citations for:

  • Direct quotes from any source
  • Statistics, data, and specific numbers
  • Controversial claims or statements
  • Technical details that aren't common knowledge
  • Historical information and dates
  • Specific methodologies or processes
  • Opinions, interpretations, or analyses from others

Selecting Reliable Sources

To maintain the quality and credibility of Kryptopedia, consider these guidelines when selecting sources:

  • Peer-reviewed journals are usually the most reliable sources for scientific or academic information
  • Choose primary sources over secondary whenever possible
  • Official documentation, whitepapers, and specifications are excellent for technical details
  • Prefer sources that are current and up-to-date, especially for rapidly evolving topics
  • Consider the expertise and reputation of the author or organization
  • Be cautious with sources that have potential conflicts of interest
Tip: When citing from websites, take a moment to evaluate the site's credibility. Look for information about the authors, the organization behind the site, and whether the content is backed by research or expert analysis.

Multiple Citations

If multiple sources support the same statement, you can group them in the same citation:

This fact is supported by multiple studies.<sup><a href="#ref1" id="cite1">[1]</a><a href="#ref2" id="cite2">[2]</a><a href="#ref3" id="cite3">[3]</a></sup>
This fact is supported by multiple studies.[1][2][3]

Consistent Formatting

Maintain consistency throughout your article by:

  • Using the same citation style for all references
  • Formatting all citations of the same type in the same way
  • Keeping the references section organized (usually alphabetical by author's last name)
  • Ensuring every citation in the text has a corresponding entry in the references section

Additional Tips

Archiving Web Sources

Web pages can change or disappear over time. To preserve the sources you cite, consider using an archiving service:

  1. Visit Internet Archive's Wayback Machine or archive.is
  2. Enter the URL of the web page you want to archive
  3. Create a new archive or select an existing snapshot
  4. Include the archive URL in your citation
Author, A. (2022). Article title. Website Name. URL [Archived at: https://web.archive.org/web/20220101000000/https://example.com/article]
Author, A. (2022). Article title. Website Name. URL [Archived at: https://web.archive.org/web/20220101000000/https://example.com/article]

Citing Primary Sources

When possible, cite primary sources rather than secondary ones. For example:

  • Cite the original research paper rather than a news article about it
  • Reference the official documentation rather than a tutorial that explains it
  • Link to the original whitepaper rather than a summary

Further Reading Section

In addition to the References section, you can include a "Further Reading" or "External Links" section for sources that aren't directly cited but provide valuable additional information:

<h2>Further Reading</h2> <ul> <li>Author, A. (Year). <em>Relevant Book Title</em>. Publisher.</li> <li>Organization Name. (Year). Useful resource title. URL</li> </ul>

Further Reading

  • Author, A. (Year). Relevant Book Title. Publisher.
  • Organization Name. (Year). Useful resource title. URL
