Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 15, 2025

At Kryptopedia, we value the privacy rights of our users. Unlike many online services, we believe in minimizing data collection and maximizing user control. This policy outlines our approach to privacy, which is guided by the same principles of decentralization and individual sovereignty that inform the blockchain technologies we document.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide Voluntarily

To create an account, we require:

  • A username (which can be pseudonymous)
  • An email address (used for account recovery and optional notifications)
  • A password (stored using cryptographic hashing)

When contributing content, you may choose to provide:

  • Article text and edits
  • Comments and discussion posts
  • Votes and content ratings

Information Collected Automatically

We collect minimal technical data necessary for the operation of our service:

  • IP addresses (temporarily stored for security purposes)
  • Browser type and version
  • Pages visited and actions taken
  • Timestamps of interactions

We use local storage and session tokens rather than traditional cookies whenever possible.

Our Philosophy on Data

Our approach to data is guided by these principles:

  • Data Minimization: We collect only what's necessary to provide our service.
  • User Control: You can delete your account and personal data at any time.
  • Pseudonymity: You can participate under a pseudonym without revealing your identity.
  • Transparency: We're clear about what we collect and how we use it.
  • Security: We employ encryption and secure storage practices.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide and maintain the Kryptopedia platform
  • Attribute content to your account and build your reputation score
  • Distribute rewards for contributions
  • Prevent abuse, spam, and malicious activity
  • Analyze usage patterns to improve the platform
  • Communicate important updates about the service

We do not:

  • Sell your personal data to third parties
  • Use your data for targeted advertising
  • Create profiles to track you across other websites
  • Share your private information with government entities without a valid legal order

Blockchain Integration and Privacy

Kryptopedia uses blockchain technology for our reward system and content verification. This means:

  • Contributions and rewards are recorded on a public blockchain
  • Your wallet address may be linked to your account if you choose to receive rewards
  • Transaction history related to content rewards is publicly visible

For enhanced privacy, we support:

  • Integration with privacy-preserving cryptocurrencies
  • Keeping your wallet addresses separate from your identity
  • Options to receive rewards without linking to personally identifiable information

Your Privacy Rights and Choices

You have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Correction: Update or correct inaccurate data
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your personal information
  • Restriction: Limit how we use your data
  • Portability: Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Objection: Oppose processing of your data for certain purposes

To exercise these rights, please contact us at privacy@kryptopedia.org.

Note: Content you've contributed to the platform will remain, attributed to "Anonymous" or a generic username, after account deletion.

Security

We implement strong security measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption of sensitive data at rest and in transit
  • Regular security audits and vulnerability testing
  • Two-factor authentication options
  • Decentralized storage to reduce central points of failure
  • Cryptographic access controls

While we strive for maximum security, no system is perfectly secure. We recommend using strong, unique passwords and enabling two-factor authentication for your account.

International Data Transfers

Kryptopedia operates globally, with data stored on decentralized nodes around the world. By using our service, you consent to the transfer of your information to countries which may have different data protection rules than your country.

We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your information regardless of location.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date.

For significant changes, we will provide more prominent notice or obtain consent as required by law.

Contact Us

If you have questions about this privacy policy or our privacy practices, please contact us at:

  • Email: privacy@kryptopedia.org
  • Telegram: @kryptopedia_privacy
  • Mail: Kryptopedia Privacy Office, 123 Blockchain Way, Suite 404, San Francisco, CA 94107
