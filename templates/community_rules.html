Community Rules

Welcome to Kryptopedia. Our community is built on collaboration, respect, and the pursuit of knowledge. These rules are designed to create a positive environment where everyone can contribute effectively. By participating in Kryptopedia, you agree to follow these guidelines.

Table of Contents

  • Core Principles
  • Content Standards
  • User Conduct
  • Collaboration Guidelines
  • Dispute Resolution
  • Rule Enforcement

Core Principles

Our community is guided by these fundamental principles:

Accuracy and Verifiability

Information shared on Kryptopedia should be factual, verifiable, and supported by reliable sources. Speculation should be clearly identified as such.

Neutrality

Content should present information from a neutral point of view, fairly representing all significant perspectives without bias.

Collaborative Spirit

Kryptopedia thrives through collaboration. Be open to working with others and having your contributions edited and improved.

Respect and Civility

Treat all community members with respect, even in disagreement. Focus on content, not contributors.

Transparency

Edits and administrative actions should be transparent, with clear explanations provided.

Content Standards

All content on Kryptopedia must adhere to these standards:

Acceptable Content

  • Factual, educational information relevant to the article topic
  • Content supported by reliable sources
  • Balanced presentation of different viewpoints
  • Original content or appropriately licensed material

Prohibited Content

  • Misinformation or deliberately misleading content
  • Unsourced claims, especially on controversial topics
  • Promotional content or advertising
  • Content that violates copyright laws
  • Personal information or private data about individuals
  • Content that promotes illegal activities
  • Hate speech, harassment, or discriminatory content
  • Obscene, vulgar, or sexually explicit material

Citations and Sources

All significant claims should be supported by reliable sources:

  • Academic publications, peer-reviewed journals, and reputable books
  • Established news organizations and industry publications
  • Official reports from governments, organizations, or companies
  • Primary source materials where appropriate

Avoid using social media posts, personal blogs, or unreliable websites as primary sources.

User Conduct

How we behave and interact with each other is as important as the content we create.

Expected Behavior

  • Assume good faith from other contributors
  • Focus on improving content rather than criticizing others
  • Provide constructive feedback in a respectful manner
  • Be open to receiving feedback and criticism
  • Use clear edit summaries to explain your changes
  • Respect the time and effort of others

Prohibited Behavior

  • Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks against others
  • Disruptive editing, including repeated reversions without discussion
  • Creating multiple accounts to bypass rules (sockpuppeting)
  • Impersonating other users or public figures
  • Deliberately introducing false or misleading information
  • Attempting to game the reward system
  • Spamming or excessive self-promotion

Communication Guidelines

  • Use clear, concise language in discussions
  • Stay on topic and focus on content issues
  • Avoid heated or aggressive language
  • Respect others' time by being efficient in communications
  • Use discussion pages for extended conversations about articles

Collaboration Guidelines

Effective collaboration is essential to Kryptopedia's success.

Editing Articles

  • Make incremental improvements rather than wholesale rewrites
  • Use the preview function before saving changes
  • Provide clear edit summaries explaining your changes
  • Check an article's history before making significant changes
  • Respect established conventions within articles

Proposing Changes

  • Use the proposal system for significant changes to established articles
  • Clearly explain the rationale behind your proposals
  • Be patient during the review process
  • Be receptive to feedback from reviewers

Reviewing Proposals

  • Evaluate changes based on content quality, not personal preference
  • Provide constructive feedback, especially when rejecting proposals
  • Focus on helping contributors improve their submissions
  • Review promptly to maintain community momentum

Dispute Resolution

When disagreements arise, follow these steps to resolve them constructively:

Step 1: Direct Discussion

Start by discussing the issue on the article's discussion page. Focus on content rather than personalities, and try to understand all perspectives.

Step 2: Seek Third Opinion

If direct discussion doesn't resolve the issue, request input from uninvolved editors through the Help Forum.

Step 3: Community Mediation

For persistent disputes, request formal mediation through the Mediation Request page. A designated mediator will help facilitate discussion.

Step 4: Administrative Review

As a last resort, administrators may be asked to review and make a determination based on community guidelines and previous consensus.

During Disputes

  • Maintain civility throughout the process
  • Avoid editing the disputed content until resolution
  • Focus on finding consensus, not "winning" the dispute
  • Take breaks if discussions become too heated

Rule Enforcement

To maintain a healthy community, these rules are enforced through a progressive system:

Warning

First-time or minor violations typically result in a warning explaining the issue and how to correct it.

Temporary Restrictions

Repeated violations or more serious infractions may result in temporary editing restrictions, such as requiring approval for edits or limits on specific actions.

Temporary Blocks

Continued violations despite warnings or restrictions may result in temporary blocks from editing, typically increasing in duration for repeat offenses.

Indefinite Blocks

Severe violations or patterns of disruptive behavior that continue despite interventions may result in indefinite blocks.

Appeals

Users who believe enforcement actions were unfair may appeal through the Appeals Process. Appeals are reviewed by uninvolved administrators.

Enforcement actions are taken with the goal of maintaining community standards while helping users become productive contributors.

These community rules are subject to change as Kryptopedia evolves. Significant changes will be announced on the Community Portal.
